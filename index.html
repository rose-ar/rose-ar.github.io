Abstract

In recent years, a number of approaches based on 3D convolutional neural networks (CNN) or Transformer based networks have emerged for video action recognition, achieving state-of-the-art results on several large-scale benchmark datasets. In this paper, we carry out in-depth comparative analysis to better understand the differences between these approaches and their robustness. Robustness has been a research topic due to its importance in real-world applications. However, most of the effort is directed towards non-sequential image data. World is inherently sequential, e.g., human beings learn different skills sequentially in a continual manner, the sequential data like video and language are the natural forms of the input to the vision systems that operate in the real world. For example perception, activity recognition, conversational systems and commonsense reasoning about physical world require sequential image and language data as input. Therefore studying robustness in sequential data is an important step towards developing reliable systems that can be deployed in the real world.
